## What each does

### Mad Lads — *confidence through emptiness*
Almost no copy. Logo, 5 nav items (Roster / Memes / Rug / Gallery / News), one "Buy" button, push to Backpack wallet. No hero paragraph, no stats bar, no roadmap on the front door. The brand is so established it doesn't explain itself. Takeaway: **the strongest brands under-explain.** A wall of lore reads as insecurity.

### Solana Monkey Business — *provenance as product*
Leads with "one of the oldest and most recognized brands on Solana." Sells **status and history**, not art. Mint mechanics written as plain prose (raffle numbers, $31M volume, refund logic). DAO (MonkeDAO) is the center of gravity. Takeaway: **receipts > promises.** Real numbers and history beat invented sci-fi lore.

### Claynosaurz — *it's a franchise, not a drop*
Frames itself as animated IP — "The Adventure Awaits," characters with personality, heavy Instagram/TikTok/YouTube presence, Sanity CMS for constant content. Warm, colorful clay 3D, not dark/neon. Takeaway: **character + story + light palette** is a proven differentiator on Solana.

### Art Blocks — *the museum model*
Homepage is a curated grid of works, each **credited to a named artist** ("DOS by William Mapan"). Light background, restrained type, art does the talking. No hype language. Takeaway: **treat the collection as fine art in a gallery**, with attribution and curation, and it reads as legitimate rather than as a cash grab.

### fxhash — *platform energy*
Explore / Editions / Analytics / Activity, live interactive art tools, a token ($FXH). Dense, utilitarian. Takeaway: less relevant for a single-collection landing, but the **artist-tooling / on-chain-generative** framing signals seriousness.

## The generic checklist (what 90% of NFT mint sites do)

Every dark Solana mint page ships the SAME kit:

1. Dark background (#0a0a0a) + one neon accent
2. Glitch / cyberpunk / "signal & static" aesthetic
3. Mystical lore ("4,444 souls", "when the chain dreamed")
4. Orbiting rings / holographic cards / scanlines / film grain
5. A stats bar: Supply / Price / Royalties / Traits
6. A scrolling marquee of trait names
7. Rarity % bars
8. A 4-card "What holders get" grid (Discord, staking, events, treasury)
9. Terminal/console fake-feed
10. Roadmap timeline with Phase 01–05

> **The current CHROMZY site does literally all 10.** That is exactly why it feels generic — it's a bingo card of the genre. It looks AI-made because the AI averaged every NFT site into one.

## Differentiation thesis → how CHROMZY wins

Go where the genre **isn't**. The whitespace in the market (literally) is **light + editorial + gallery**.

**Position CHROMZY as a generative-art collection shown like a museum print magazine — not a crypto mint.**

| Genre default | CHROMZY move |
|---|---|
| Dark + neon | **Warm off-white paper**, ink-black type, 1–2 restrained ink accents |
| Glitch/cyberpunk | **Editorial / Swiss print / gallery** — big type, rules, generous margins |
| Mystical lore wall | **Plain confident copy + real receipts** (supply, artist, on-chain facts) |
| Holo cards + rings + grain | **Art-forward grid**, the work framed like plates in a catalogue |
| Stats bar + marquee + rarity bars | **One quiet spec table**, set like a colophon |
| Anonymous hype | **Named artist attribution** (Art Blocks model) — credibility |
| 10 effects competing | **One signature interaction**, used once, memorably |

This is defensible: light editorial is rare in PFP/NFT, it photographs well for social, and it signals "art" over "ape." It also reads as deliberately hand-designed — the opposite of the averaged-AI look.

## Concrete patterns worth KEEPING (validated by competitors)

- Above-fold clarity: chain, price, supply visible before wallet connect (all do this)
- Progressive wallet: connect only on mint intent (Mad Lads)
- Separate focused mint page (all)
- Real numbers / provenance over promises (SMB)
- Named-artist attribution + curation (Art Blocks)
- Strong social funnel (Claynosaurz)

## Anti-patterns to DROP from current build

Dark theme, glitch title, scanlines, grain, orbiting rings, terminal feed, trait marquee, the 4-cell utility grid clichés, "4,444 souls" sci-fi lore voice, teal-on-lime color confusion.
